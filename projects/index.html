Mixture Cure Model Link to heading

@ June, 2022 - April, 2023

Brachytherapy is a common treatment for cervical cancer patients, crucially in reducing the recurrence rate of patients and improving patients’ prognosis. Quite a few patients are considered to be cured effectively after treatment. However, traditional survivsl methods assume those who are censored have the same prognosis as those who remain under observation. These methods are unable to deal with unobserved cure patients in the censored data.

The mixture cure model is specifically developed to handle the challenges. In the mixture cure model, a portion of the subjects are deemed immune from the outcome of interest $(1-\pi(z))$; the outcome of these subjects will never be observed no matter how long the follow-up time is.

$$ S(t|z,x) = 1-\pi(z)+\pi(z)S_{\mu}(t|x) $$

  • Conducted the mixture cox PH cure model in mixcure package to explore the factors for recurrence time and factors for cured probability respectively, and estimated the cured fractions.
  • Identified the interaction effect of age and activity of radioactive source (ARS) after propensity score matching, demonstrating that high ARS played opposite roles in the cure probability in young group and old group innovatively.
